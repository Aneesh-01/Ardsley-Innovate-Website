About

Ardsley Hacks is the fourth hackathon Ardsley High School is hosting. High schoolers, of any experience level, can participate. You can form teams of 1-5 students, even across different schools, and work to make projects relating to coding. We'll have workshops and mentors to help you with your projects and learn! We will announce the surprise theme a week before the competition, after which you can start coding, and continue until 4 pm on Saturday. You’ll present your projects to our panels of judges to win prizes in multiple categories!


Theme

The theme will be announced a week prior to the hackathon. Once it is announced, you will be able to begin your project with your team. Stay tuned!


Awards


Written Code

This award is for people who have the best written code. Code should be readable, well-structured, well-organized, reliable, and efficient.

Overall Project

This award is for having the best project as a whole. Projects should be innovative and ready to use. Simply the best!

Beginner Category

This award is for those who have not been to any hackathons. We welcome coders of all experiences!

F.A.Q.


What is a hackathon?
A hackathon is an event where teams get together and build a project relating to coding, using software or hardware components, in a short period of time.

Who can come to Ardsley Hacks?
Anyone! Everyone is welcome no matter their prior coding knowledge. Hackers must be high schoolers and bring one adult chaperone from their school for insurance purposes.

Is there a theme for Ardsley Hacks?
We will be announcing the theme a week before the Hackathon. When we announce the theme, teams are encouraged to begin working on their projects.
